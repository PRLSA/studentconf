What is This Symposium?

The 2023 Postgraduate Research Symposium on Linguistics and Language Data Analytics, to be collocated with PACLIC 2023 from Dec 2nd to Dec 5th, aims to offer an inter-institutional platform for local and international postgraduate students and researchers in linguistics-related fields using data-driven approaches and methodologies. They are cordially invited and encouraged to: • Discuss their current progress and prospective work in their interested topics, • Share their experience and expertise in undertaking data-driven research, • Exchange innovative ideas about language data acquisition and language source construction, • Communicate with our invited scholars about low-resource language, language typology, and the representation of formal grammar in natural language processing and psycholinguistics. The essential objective of this symposium is to encourage novel ideas from the next generation of researchers, inter-institutional and inter-disciplinary contacts, and collaborations among UGC and non-UGC-funded university research students. The fields of interest include but are not limited to the application of Corpus Linguistics in Lexical Semantics, Natural Language Processing, the linguistic probe of Computational Language Models, Language Resource Construction, Cognitive Language Processing, Ontology, Metaphor, and Data Humanities. Our proposed student symposium will last for three days. Two seminar series will help attendees identify research topics and address the research-related difficulties encountered by research students. During the two seminar events, Prof.Yang and Prof.Lori, two of the most top-level and productive researchers in both language technologies and theoretic linguistics, will be invited to give academic talks to postgraduate students invited from local or nonlocal universities, sharing their research experience in multilingual language data analysis, the probing of generative linguistics in distributional language representation, and the conservation of endangered languages using artificial intelligence.
